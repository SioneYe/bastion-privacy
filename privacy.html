Privacy Policy

How Bastion Command handles your data and network activity.

Last updated: July 18, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Bastion Command ("Bastion Command", "the Application", "we", or "us") processes information when you use the Application. By using Bastion Command, you acknowledge the practices described below.

1. Scope

This Policy applies to the Bastion Command mobile and local web application distributed for personal use on your device. It does not create an account service, cloud hosting relationship, or remote data processing service for your personal life records.

2. No personal data collection

Bastion Command does not collect, transmit, upload, or share your personal, financial, health, journal, relationship, or other life-management data with the developer or any third party. All such information resides strictly in on-device local SQLite storage under your control. Uninstalling the Application, or performing an in-app data wipe (including optional duress protocols), removes that local data from the device according to the platform and feature behavior.

3. Local storage and processing

Features such as finance tracking, health logs, encrypted Memory Vault entries, backups you create, and related modules read and write data only on your device. Local processing may include on-device calculations, reminders, and security checks. These operations do not require a Bastion Command cloud backend.

4. Network activity

The Application is Offline-First. Network requests are made solely to obtain public cryptocurrency market data for the Digital Assets module (currently live Kaspa price information via the public CoinGecko API). Those requests do not include your holdings, wallet addresses, portfolio values, PIN, biometric material, backup files, or any other personal identifiers. All other modules are designed to function without network connectivity.

5. No analytics or advertising

Bastion Command does not include third-party analytics, advertising, attribution, crash-reporting, or tracking software development kits (SDKs). The Application does not sell personal information and does not use advertising identifiers for marketing.

6. Data security

Security controls available in the Application include a global App Lock (PIN and optional device biometrics), encrypted export/restore of local database backups, and optional duress protocols that can wipe local application data. You are responsible for safeguarding your PIN, master passwords, biometric enrollment on the device, and any backup files you export. No security measure is perfect; you use the Application at your own risk with respect to device compromise, lost credentials, or lost backup media.

7. Permissions

Depending on your platform and the features you enable, Bastion Command may request permissions such as biometric authentication, local notifications, and network access required for public market-data lookups. Permissions are used only for the stated on-device features.

8. Children's privacy

Bastion Command is not directed to children under the age of 13 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction). Do not use the Application to store information about children if applicable law restricts such use.

9. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect product or legal changes. Material updates will be reflected by revising the "Last updated" date in the Application. Continued use after an update constitutes acknowledgement of the revised Policy.

10. Contact

For privacy questions regarding Bastion Command, contact the developer through the support channel listed on the Google Play store listing for this Application.
